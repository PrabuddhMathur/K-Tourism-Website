Things to do in Kerala

Catamaran Cruise

Kovalam is a favourite beach destination for travellers around the world. This coastal town in Kerala invites you to experience some exciting activities. You can begin your day here on a high note by hopping onto a catamaran and rowing into the sea. For incredible views of Kerala coastal life, you must sail along the vast stretch of the blue ocean world and join your hands with the fisherfolks. The catamaran cruise is a thrilling activity to engage in while visiting God's Own Country.

Festivals

The festival season, the pride and joy of Kerala, is epitomised by the ferocious beats of the chenda (drums) emanating across the landscape. Elephants march across towns and the rivers surge with massive 100 feet long snake boats in a display that can only be experienced in an area renowned as God's Own Country. The entire State celebrates together as the land is covered in a plethora of lights and merriment.

Fishing

To know the spirit of Kerala, turn to the livelihood practices of the people in this land that collectively forms the backbone of this state. Fishing, more than just a livelihood, has grabbed the attention of tourists seeking to learn the nuances of the practice.

Malabar Cruisine

Flanked by the Western Ghats and the Arabian Sea, the Malabar area is responsible for having provided some of the most delicious and flavourful additions to Kerala cuisine. It has a wide range of vegetarian and non-vegetarian fare and people flock to these parts to taste the unique flavour of the food here. Here is a collection of recipes of some of the premier dishes that best represent Malabar’s food culture. The very aroma of Malabar dishes will surely transport you to the friendly shores from which this delicious cuisine originated. Enjoy cooking.

Adventure Activities

You will certainly get mesmerised with Kollam’s latest tourist attraction, the Jatayu Earth’s Center that hosts the world’s largest bird sculpture. Spanning over 65 acres across four hills, this is the State’s first ever BOT (Build-Operate-Transfer) tourism. The monumental statue of Jatayu resting high up the hill catches your attention even from afar on your way to Chadayamangalam in Kollam district. Jatayu is a legendary bird from the Indian epic Ramayana.
